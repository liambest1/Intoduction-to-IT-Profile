My real interest in Information Technology didn’t start until I was 20 in my second year of university
when I went to a friend's house to study and he was showing me the PC he had built. After that, I had become
obsessed with building my own, every night I watch PC building tutorials or reading forums on parts and advice.
After I got my first role as an IT professional I became fascinated by the network administration component
whether it was user administration in Active Directory or building and establishing and new server.
I have always had a curiosity for programming but without the structure of a formal lesson found it difficult
to motivate myself to learn. I enjoy working face to face with people and moving around during
the day so my role as onsite Technical support has been well suited to me.

My friend in my first year of university was the first influence I had on the world of Information Technology,
before that I had only used a computer as a standard user with the most technical use was torrenting
(open-source software of course). After finally saving to build my own PC I didn’t do much else in regards to IT
for another year. I owe my start in IT to my step-dad who is a client manager in IT, my first job in IT was offered
to me by him at the Manage Service Provider he worked at. In this role, I was a self-employed contractor doing a
company-wide roll-out of new laptops for REA Group (Realestate.com) using images pre-made and using PXE boot to
install them. this was my first taste of professional IT. That role only lasted 4 months but was enough to impress
the boss and I was offered a role of level 1 service desk operator for several remote clients and 2 days a week
onsite assistance to one client located in far rural Victoria. In this job, I did a variety of work from managing
site backups to replacing broken keyboards. After this, I got my job now at the Moonee Valley Racing Club where I
provide almost 24-hour support to the staff at the club, 3 gaming venues as well as point of sale network assistance
at the Melbourne Show Grounds. My current role has slowly become a level 1 and 2 role as I do more work building and
implementing servers, switches and the total implementation of multi-site monitoring software while still working with
staff to maintain business as usual tech support.

I had applied to RMIT for several reasons. First, I got a recommendation from a current student who was also studying
online with RMIT and had a great experience with the learning style. After plenty of research, I knew I would have to study
online to make sure that I could manage full-time work and study part-time, this meant to me that Open Universities Australia
was the best path for applying and RMIT was available there. RMIT offers this robust course in Information Technology allowing
me to learn all the fundamentals that I have not been able to pick up with on the job learning, along with minor subjects in
Management which I believe will be very useful in day-to-day operations. This course will also prepare in fields of IT that,
while I might not focus on, will allow me to start implementing it and be prepared when I do come across these issues, such as programming the security.
